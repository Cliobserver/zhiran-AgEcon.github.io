With Sarah Consoli, Elizabeth A. Fraysse, Natalya Slipchenko, Yi Wang, Jahon Amirebrahimi, Neil Yazma, and Travis J. Lybbert. This paper explores grower supply responses to the demand shock triggered by the 2004 release of the movie *Sideways*. Using a modified difference-in-difference approach and USDA data for 1999–2012, we find evidence of asymmetric supply responses: a stronger positive response for Pinot Noir concentrated in lower-value Central Valley vineyards, and a weaker negative response for Merlot.
